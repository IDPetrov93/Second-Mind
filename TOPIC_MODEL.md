# TOPIC MODEL

## Purpose

A Topic is the primary unit of maintained knowledge in KOS.

KOS does not maintain news.

KOS maintains Topics.

Incoming information exists only to create, enrich or update the understanding of a Topic.

---

# Definition

A Topic represents a coherent subject whose state evolves over time.

A Topic is not defined by a single event.

A Topic is defined by continuous knowledge.

Examples:

- Bitcoin
- Ethereum
- Artificial Intelligence
- NVIDIA
- Federal Reserve
- Tokenized Assets
- Quantum Computing

---

# Lifecycle

Every Topic follows the same lifecycle.

```
Bootstrap

↓

Knowledge Acquisition

↓

Knowledge Maintenance

↓

Knowledge Synthesis

↓

Continuous Updates
```

Topics never become "finished".

Knowledge continuously evolves.

---

# Bootstrap

When a Topic is created, KOS builds an initial understanding.

Bootstrap should collect enough trusted information to understand:

- what the Topic is;
- why it matters;
- who the important Entities are;
- which Sources are authoritative;
- historical context;
- terminology;
- major events;
- current state.

Bootstrap is performed only once.

---

# Knowledge Acquisition

Every new Signal may introduce new knowledge.

KOS gathers:

- official sources;
- independent reporting;
- previous related knowledge;
- quantitative information;
- contradicting evidence;
- supporting evidence;
- historical information.

The objective is understanding.

Not confirmation.

---

# Knowledge Maintenance

KOS continuously monitors whether existing knowledge has changed.

Possible outcomes:

- no change;
- additional context;
- updated fact;
- corrected information;
- deprecated knowledge.

Knowledge remains internally consistent over time.

---

# Knowledge Synthesis

At any moment KOS should be able to explain:

- what is currently true;
- what recently changed;
- why it matters;
- what evidence supports it;
- where uncertainty remains.

Knowledge Synthesis represents the current understanding of the Topic.

---

# Signals

Signals are temporary.

Topics are persistent.

Signals trigger investigation.

Topics accumulate knowledge.

Signals never become the primary object inside KOS.

---

# Relationship with Knowledge Objects

A Topic contains many Knowledge Objects.

Knowledge Objects may include:

- Statements
- Claims
- Evidence
- Entities
- Relations
- Observations

Knowledge Objects never exist without belonging to at least one Topic.

---

# Outputs

Outputs never consume Signals directly.

Outputs consume Topics.

This guarantees:

- consistency;
- traceability;
- historical continuity;
- contextual awareness.

---

# Core Principle

KOS answers questions about Topics.

Never about isolated headlines.

The objective is to maintain an evolving understanding rather than an archive of information.
