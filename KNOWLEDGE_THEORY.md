# KNOWLEDGE THEORY

## Purpose

This document defines what Knowledge means within KOS.

It establishes the principles that distinguish Information from Knowledge and defines how the Core transforms information into progressively better knowledge.

This document is conceptual.

It defines neither architecture nor implementation.

---

# First Principle

KOS does not collect knowledge.

KOS collects information.

Knowledge is constructed.

---

# Information vs Knowledge

Information is an observation received from the outside world.

Knowledge is the current best understanding produced from evaluating many pieces of information.

Information exists independently.

Knowledge exists only inside KOS.

---

# Knowledge is a Representation

Knowledge is not reality.

Knowledge is not truth.

Reality exists independently of KOS.

Knowledge is only an internal representation of reality.

Knowledge is the current best representation of reality that KOS can construct using the available information.

As new information becomes available, this representation may improve or degrade.

---

# Knowledge is Dynamic

Knowledge is never finished.

Every new piece of information has the potential to:

- strengthen existing knowledge
- weaken existing knowledge
- contradict existing knowledge
- expand existing knowledge
- replace existing knowledge

Knowledge continuously evolves.

---

# Knowledge is Traceable

Every element of Knowledge must be explainable.

For every conclusion, KOS must be able to answer:

- Which information contributed?
- Which sources produced that information?
- Which claims were evaluated?
- Which evidence influenced confidence?
- Why is this the current representation?

Knowledge without traceability is invalid.

---

# Knowledge is Never Absolute

KOS never claims certainty.

Knowledge always represents the current state of understanding.

Confidence expresses reliability.

Confidence never represents truth.

---

# Knowledge Improves Through Evaluation

The Core continuously evaluates new information.

Evaluation may change:

- confidence
- relationships
- context
- interpretations

Original information is never modified.

Only the representation of knowledge evolves.

---

# Human-Centered Knowledge

The purpose of Knowledge is not to replace human reasoning.

Its purpose is to improve human reasoning.

Knowledge should help humans:

- understand
- compare
- discover
- verify
- question

The final decision always belongs to the human.

---

# Self-Improvement

KOS does not autonomously redefine reality.

KOS continuously improves its internal representation of knowledge as additional information becomes available.

Improvement is achieved through:

- validation
- comparison
- contradiction detection
- relationship discovery
- confidence reassessment
- contextual enrichment

The Core evolves its representation.

It does not evolve its principles.

---

# Core Objective

The objective of KOS is not information storage.

The objective of KOS is not document management.

The objective of KOS is not search.

The objective of KOS is to transform continuously growing information into continuously improving knowledge.

---

# Definition

Knowledge is the current best explainable representation of reality that KOS can construct from available information, supported by traceable evidence, contextual understanding and measurable confidence.

This representation is expected to evolve as new information becomes available.

Knowledge is never final.

Knowledge is never absolute.

Knowledge exists to improve human understanding.
